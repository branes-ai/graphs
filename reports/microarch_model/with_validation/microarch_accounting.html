KPU Compute Tile (32x32 FMA mesh)

Native op: Mesh steady-state: 1024 MACs per clock (32x32 PEs, 1 MAC/PE/clock)
Process: 8 nm | Clock: 1.50 GHz | MACs / native op: 1,024 | Silicon: 22 M transistors | 1-bit FA reference: 0.005 pJ

Tile-internal accounting. Does NOT include L3 scratchpad, memory controller, or DRAM. The absence of instruction fetch, decode, warp scheduling, coherence, packet routers, and schedule ROMs is structural - those components do not exist in a 2D-mesh domain-flow fabric. That absence is the KPU's architectural efficiency story.

Cross-validation against simplified view (generalized_architecture.py) at matched 8 nm:
SM (Tensor Core) detailed total: 0.171 pJ/MAC. Simplified: 0.150 pJ/MAC. Delta: 14%.
KPU tile detailed total: 0.130 pJ/MAC. Simplified: 0.086 pJ/MAC. Delta: 51%.
SM/KPU ratio at 8 nm: detailed 1.31x, simplified 1.73x.

Totals agree within ~50%; the detailed view explicitly adds a leakage term (15-20% of dynamic) and itemizes smaller structures (clock tree, token-match, pipeline latches, LDSM) that the simplified model rolls up. Most important: the SM/KPU ratio is consistent across both views, validating the architectural-advantage story independent of model granularity.
What this view covers. SM-internal and tile-internal structures only. External memory hierarchy (L2 cache, HBM / LPDDR) is out of scope - those are Layer 4-7 of the bottom-up plan and are covered separately.

What the absences mean. The KPU tile table has no rows for instruction fetch, decode, warp scheduling, or coherence - those structures do not exist in a domain-flow fabric. The energy saved by their absence is not "efficiency" in the colloquial sense; it is the absence of work the architecture declines to do per MAC.

Silicon composition: ALU -> PE -> Tile -> Cluster -> SoC

Companion to the building-block energy and per-MAC accounting views. Where those report the silicon-capability ceiling at the ALU level, this page follows the scaffolding tax as you wrap an ALU in successively larger composition levels. The drop in TOPS/W from ALU to SoC is the "overhead for coordination" that SoC specialization tries to minimize against a given workload's op mix.

How to read these tables

  • TOPS/W is silicon efficiency at that level (ops_per_MAC / pJ_per_MAC). It drops as you climb the hierarchy because more transistors serve coordination rather than compute.
  • Active % is the fraction of the level's transistors that participate in MAC computation on a dense INT8 workload. A CPU core sits near 0.5%; a KPU tile is above 70%.
  • Efficiency vs ALU shows how far the block's TOPS/W is below its own ALU's ceiling.
  • Decay in the arch header is the ratio ALU TOPS/W / SoC TOPS/W. A 50x decay means the SoC delivers 1/50 the silicon efficiency of its own bare ALU.

Cross-architecture efficiency decay

TOPS/W at each composition level, log-scale y-axis. Architectures that keep their TOPS/W flat from ALU to SoC are less wasteful of silicon on coordination overhead.

SoC specialization pointer: The composition hierarchy is the input to workload-driven SoC design. Given a workload's mix of (matmul / scatter-gather / sequential-control) operations, the right SoC combines CPU cores, DSP lanes, GPU SMs, and NPU tiles in proportions that match the op mix. This view provides the per-level silicon efficiency that feeds that trade-off analysis.
